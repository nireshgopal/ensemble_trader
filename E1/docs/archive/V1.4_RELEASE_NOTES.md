# Strategy E1 Specification Update: V1.4

## 1. Regime-Gated Stop Expansion
**Change**: Increase stop width from 6.0x ATR to 8.0x ATR in BEAR regime only.

| Market Regime | ATR Stop Multiplier | Rationale |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **HEALTHY** | 6.0x | Unchanged. Standard volatility buffer. |
| **FRAGILE** | 7.0x | Explicitly defined intermediate buffer for increasing variance. |
| **BEAR**    | 8.0x | Max buffer for bear-volatility shakeouts in quality names. |

**Rationale**: 2022 forensic rerun confirmed validated evidence of improvement in the bear stress test while preserving trade count. Sector RS veto was rejected because it collapsed sample size and conflicted with the strategy’s bear-regime design. 

## 2. Liquidity & Volume Governance
A **Sector RS Veto** was explored as an alternative but **REJECTED**.
- **Issue**: RS Veto reduced 2022 trade count by 77% (from 50+ to 10), rendering the strategy statistically inoperable.
- **Decision**: Volatility survival via stop expansion is preferred over entry blocking to maintain strategy liquidity.

## 3. Residual Tail Risk Disclosure
- **EXC Gap-Down (-29%)**: Identified as a structural "Black Swan" event. 
- **Disclaimer**: No ATR stop width (even 10.0x+) can block an overnight gap-down of this magnitude. This is documented as the known residual tail risk of the strategy.
